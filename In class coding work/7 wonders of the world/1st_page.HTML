The Great Pyramids at Giza:

The Great Pyramids of Giza are the oldest of the 7 Wonders of the World and were present in the first list of wonders of the wonders.

  • The Great Pyramid was the tallest man-made building for over 3,800 years
  • The largest of the Pyramids consists of 2.3 million stones each weighing between 2.5 and 15 tons
  • The three largest pyramids present were built by three succesive kings of the 4th Egyptian Dynasty

The Great Wall of China:

The Great Wall of China is the largest of the Great Wonders spanning much of Northern China, originally constructed to repel attacks from the norther tribes.

  • Contrary to popular belief the Great Wall cannot be seen from space
  • The Great Wall is not a single wall and consists of many sections of wall that were built in many different centuries
  • The wall spans over 21,000 kilometers which is equivalent to half of the circumference of the Earth

Petra:

Petra is built into pure sandstone and is a truly marvellous feat of engineering.

  • Possibly built as early as the 5th century BC, Petra had a whole water-conduit system
  • The city represents a coalesence of Eastern traditions and Hellenistic architecture
  • The site became a majot hub for the silk road where products from all over the old world would be routed through

Chichen Itza:

One of the greatest Pre-Columbian cities of the New World, Chichen Itza was a centre of Mayan Civilisation

  • The city of Chichen-Itza was a hub of the Mayan-Toltec civilisation
  • The blend of the Mayan and Toltec civilisations in Chichen Itza provide a unique glimpse into the peoples of the Yucatan
  • The monuents of Chichen-Itza exerted a large influence over the Yucatan peninsula

Machu Picchu:

Amongst the Andes, suspended amongst the clouds, lies the great Incan city of Machu Picchu.

  • Residing over 2 kilometers above sea level, the city of Machu Picchu is located amongst the Andes mountains
  • Located along the periphery of the boundary between the Amazon and the Andes the city and its surrounding areas consist of many micro-climates
  • As a testimate to the ingenious of the Inca, the city follows a rigorous plan dividing the agricultural and residential protions of the city

The Colosseum:

The Colosseum has been an integral part of the landscape of Rome for centuries and is a towering behemoth of Concrete and limestone

  • The Colosseum once held grand naval battles within its walls, it would be flooded with water.
  • The Colosseum could once hold 50,000 people within its walls
  • The Colosseum began construction within the year 72 AD under the emperor Vespasian
